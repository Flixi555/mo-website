Moritz S. Graefrath

I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Political Science at the University of Notre Dame, a Graduate Fellow with the Notre Dame International Security Center, and co-editor of the Europe in the World project at the Nanovic Institute for European Studies.

My research operates at the intersection of IR theory and international security, focusing on theory-driven foreign policy analysis and conceptual innovation. In my dissertation, I examine the role of power vacuums in international politics, seeking to explain both what power vacuums are and how great powers respond to their emergence. You can find out more about the project here.

In addition to my primary work on power vacuums, I have conducted research on the concept of cooperation in IR, which has appeared or is forthcoming in International Theory.

I hold an M.A. in Political Science from the University of Notre Dame and a B.A. in Philosophy & Economics from the University of Bayreuth, Germany.
